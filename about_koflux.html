ABOUT THE KoFLUX Network

KoFLUX is an international 'network of networks' connecting regional networks established to measure and understand the exchange of carbon dioxide, water, and energy between the terrestrial ecosystem and the atmosphere. KoFLUX scientists use Eddy Covariance techniques to collect data for a better understanding of the carbon cycle, water, energy, and related trace gas dynamics.

Eddy covariance is the standard method for directly measuring gas fluxes between the ecosystem and the atmosphere. It allows for non-intrusive measurement of the exchange of carbon dioxide, water, and methane. KoFLUX data is essential for improving climate change predictive models and studying the role of ecosystems.

KoFLUX Data Sharing and Activities

Researchers collaborate to process and share data through regional network data portals. KoFLUX started in 2005 and currently operates over 50 active flux measurement sites. KoFLUX provides users with data documentation, tools, and opportunities to communicate with the FLUXNET community.
